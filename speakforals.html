Focus on the Idea, Not the Execution

During my internship at Bartle Bogle Hegarty New York (BBH), we were presented with the brief to "Do Good, Famously". This three word challenge was daunting. No defined constraints brought too much creative freedom. We spent a good chunk of our 10-weeks brainstorming hundreds of ideas.

I quickly learned the importance of the discovery stage in design - validate ideas before dedicating resources to execution.

When You Can't Speak, Words Mean Everything

Our team chose to raise awareness and money for amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), an incurable disease. One of the symptoms of ALS is speech loss. We focused our idea around a universal truth - when you can't speak, words mean a lot.

We asked four people living with ALS what they wanted to say to a loved one.

"...the only thing I regret is not being able to sing to Jocelyn like I did before. Whenever we listen to Twinkle Twinkle, I cry and so does she."
"...to my three sons: We began our journey together over twenty years ago. Along the way, we skipped and tripped. Some steps were tentative, others bold and forward...carry these close to your hearts."
