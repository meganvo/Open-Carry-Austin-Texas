Validate the idea, then the execution

During my internship at Bartle Bogle Hegarty(BBH), we were challenged with a three word creative brief -"Do Good, Famously". No defined constraints brought too much creative freedom. We spent a good chunk of our 10-weeks brainstorming hundreds of ideas.

I quickly learned the importance of the discovery stage in design - validate ideas before dedicating resources to execution.

When you can't speak, words mean eveyrthing

Our team chose to raise awareness and money for amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), an incurable disease. One of the symptoms of ALS is speech loss. We focused our idea around a universal truth:

When you can't speak, words mean everything.

We worked with the ALS Association of New York who connected us to four people living with ALS. We asked wanted to say to a loved one.
