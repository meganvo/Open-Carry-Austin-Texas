Working with the National Football League (NFL), I designed an interactive data visualization experience that was fueled by social media statistics. This increased fan engagement during the draft season.

Adding Draft Picks

Typing triggers a fuzzy search to populate players. Once a player is selected, stats are generated.

Mobile first design matters

My initial iterations split up adding players between viewing the statistics. I thought it would be too cluttered to combine the two UIs.

However, I quickly learned after my initial mobile wires iterations that there was a lack of instant feedback validation once a user inputted a topic. Data would populate below the mobile viewport and it wasn't clear to the user that an action occured. With quick internal testing, I observed the cumbersome behavior of scrolling all the way down to view stats and then scrolling up to view the graph.
